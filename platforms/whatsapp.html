WhatsApp

Practical privacy actions you can take today on WhatsApp.

Navigation Primer

Mobile (WhatsApp)

  • Settings: iPhone: bottom-right tab. Android: tap ⋮ (top-right) → Settings.
  • Account: Houses security, two-step verification, and request info controls.
  • Privacy: Inside Settings, controls visibility, groups, status, and disappearing messages.
  • Linked devices: Manage computers and tablets connected to your account.

Desktop (WhatsApp Desktop/Web)

  • Open the WhatsApp desktop app or web.whatsapp.com and link your phone if needed.
  • Click your profile picture or the ⋮ menu → Settings.
  • Desktop mirrors many privacy toggles but some (like backups) remain phone-only.
  • Use Log out under Linked Devices when you finish on shared machines.

If you cannot find a control on desktop, open WhatsApp on your phone—mobile settings always take precedence.

Guides #1–12

Enable WhatsApp Two-Step Verification

On Mobile
  1. Open WhatsApp → Settings → Account.
  2. Tap Two-step verification → Turn on.
  3. Create a 6-digit PIN and add an email address for recovery.
On Desktop
  1. Open WhatsApp Desktop/Web → ⋮ menu → Settings → Account.
  2. Select Two-step verification and follow the prompts (you may be asked to finish on your phone).

Tips: Two-step verification blocks attackers even if they hijack your SMS code.

Update WhatsApp Two-Step Email

On Mobile
  1. WhatsApp → Settings → Account → Two-step verification.
  2. Tap Change email address.
  3. Enter a private email you control, confirm it, then tap Save.
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web → Settings → Account → Two-step verification.
  2. Choose Change email and update it (desktop may prompt you to finish on mobile).

Tips: Keeping the recovery email current ensures you can disable the PIN if you forget it.

Turn On End-to-End Encrypted Backups

On Mobile
  1. WhatsApp → Settings → Chats → Chat backup.
  2. Tap End-to-end encrypted backup → Turn on.
  3. Create a password or 64-digit encryption key and confirm.
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web will direct you to your phone: open WhatsApp on mobile and follow the steps above.

Tips: Encrypted backups ensure that cloud providers cannot read your message history.

Set a Default Disappearing Messages Timer

On Mobile
  1. WhatsApp → Settings → Privacy.
  2. Tap Default message timer.
  3. Select a duration (24 hours, 7 days, 90 days) for new chats.
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web → Settings → Privacy → Default message timer.
  2. Choose your preferred auto-delete window.

Tips: Disappearing messages keep new chats from storing long-term history by default.

Control WhatsApp Last Seen and Online Status

On Mobile
  1. WhatsApp → Settings → Privacy → Last seen and online.
  2. Set Last seen to My contacts or Nobody.
  3. Choose Same as last seen for Who can see when I'm online.
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web → Settings → Privacy.
  2. Adjust Last seen and online to the strictest options.

Tips: Limiting online status prevents contacts from tracking your activity patterns.

Limit Who Sees Your Profile Photo

On Mobile
  1. WhatsApp → Settings → Privacy → Profile photo.
  2. Select My contacts or My contacts except… to hide from specific people.
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web → Settings → Privacy → Profile photo.
  2. Pick who can view your photo and confirm.

Tips: Restricting your profile photo minimizes what unknown numbers see when they message you.

Limit Who Can Add You to Groups

On Mobile
  1. WhatsApp → Settings → Privacy → Groups.
  2. Select My contacts or My contacts except….
  3. When set to My contacts except…, you can leave only trusted contacts allowed to add you directly.
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web → Settings → Privacy → Groups.
  2. Choose the same restrictions as on mobile.

Tips: Restricting group invites keeps spammers from adding you without approval.

Limit Your Status Audience

On Mobile
  1. WhatsApp → Status tab → Privacy (iPhone: Privacy button; Android: ⋮ → Status privacy).
  2. Select My contacts, My contacts except…, or Only share with….
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web → Status → Status privacy.
  2. Set the audience to your preferred group.

Tips: Narrow status visibility to reduce exposure of photos or updates to casual contacts.

Lock WhatsApp with Biometric or Passcode

On Mobile
  1. WhatsApp → Settings → Privacy.
  2. Scroll to Screen lock (iPhone) or Fingerprint lock (Android).
  3. Toggle it on and choose how quickly the app locks after inactivity.
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web currently relies on your computer's lock screen. Use a strong device password and log out when done.

Tips: App locking prevents people with physical access to your phone from reading chats.

Manage Linked Devices on WhatsApp

On Mobile
  1. WhatsApp → Settings → Linked devices.
  2. Review each device and tap it → Log out if you do not recognize it.
  3. Use Link a device only on trusted computers.
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web → ⋮ menu → Log out (to disconnect the current computer).
  2. For other devices, check your phone under Linked devices and remove them there.

Tips: Regularly pruning devices stops old computers from re-opening your chats.

Clear Old Chat Backups

On Mobile
  1. iPhone: Settings app → Apple ID → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → Backups → delete outdated WhatsApp backups.
  2. Android: Open Google Drive → Backups → find WhatsApp backups → Delete backup.
  3. Return to WhatsApp → Settings → Chats → Chat backup to confirm encryption is still on.
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web cannot manage cloud backups; use your phone's iCloud or Google Drive settings instead.

Tips: Removing old unencrypted backups reduces what attackers could restore.

Request Your WhatsApp Account Info

On Mobile
  1. WhatsApp → Settings → Account → Request account info.
  2. Tap Request report. WhatsApp will prepare it within about 3 days.
  3. Return to this screen to download the report before the expiry date.
On Desktop
  1. Desktop/Web shows a shortcut but requires completing the request on your phone.

Tips: Reviewing the report reveals metadata WhatsApp keeps so you can adjust settings accordingly.
