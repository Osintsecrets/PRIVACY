Instagram

Practical privacy actions you can take today on Instagram.

Navigation Primer

Mobile (Instagram app)

  • Profile tab: Bottom-right circle icon. Tap it to open your profile.
  • Menu (☰): Top-right of your profile. Tap to open Settings and privacy.
  • Accounts Center: Lives at the top of Settings and privacy. Use it for password, login, and cross-app controls.
  • Search bar: Within Settings, use the search field at the top to find features quickly.

Desktop (instagram.com)

  • Go to instagram.com and sign in.
  • More (left sidebar, bottom) → Settings to open the settings panel.
  • Accounts Center: Appears in the left column; click it for password and security options.
  • Use the left navigation in Settings for privacy, security, and ads controls.

If the layout changes, use the Settings search field and type the feature name (e.g., “two-factor”, “private account”).

Guides #1–12

Change Your Instagram Password

On Mobile
  1. Open the Instagram app and tap your profile (bottom-right).
  2. Tap the Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy → Accounts Center.
  3. Choose Password and security → Change password.
  4. Select your Instagram account, enter your current password, set a new strong password, then tap Save.
On Desktop
  1. Visit instagram.com, sign in, then click More (left sidebar) → Settings.
  2. Select Accounts Center → Password and security → Change password.
  3. Enter your current password and a new unique password, then choose Save.

Tips: Use at least 12 characters and avoid reusing passwords from other services.

Enable Instagram Two-Factor Authentication

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy → Accounts Center.
  2. Select Password and security → Two-factor authentication.
  3. Choose Authentication app or Security key, follow the setup prompts, then tap Turn on.
On Desktop
  1. instagram.com → More → Settings → Accounts Center.
  2. Password and security → Two-factor authentication.
  3. Pick your preferred method (authenticator app or security key) and complete the flow.

Tips: Authenticator apps or security keys are more resistant to phishing than SMS codes.

Save Instagram Backup Codes

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy → Accounts Center.
  2. Tap Password and security → Two-factor authentication → Backup codes.
  3. Generate a new set, then copy or screenshot the codes and store them securely offline.
On Desktop
  1. From instagram.com, open More → Settings → Accounts Center.
  2. Choose Password and security → Two-factor authentication → Backup codes.
  3. Download or print the codes and keep them in a safe place.

Tips: Use backup codes when you do not have your phone or authenticator app available.

Review Instagram Login Activity

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy → Accounts Center.
  2. Select Password and security → Where you're logged in.
  3. Review each device; tap any unfamiliar session → Log out.
On Desktop
  1. instagram.com → More → Settings → Accounts Center.
  2. Open Password and security → Where you're logged in.
  3. Click Log out next to devices you do not recognize.

Tips: Check this after travel or using public computers to catch unwanted access quickly.

Update Instagram Recovery Info

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy → Accounts Center.
  2. Tap Personal details → Contact info.
  3. Add a recovery email and phone, verify them, then adjust visibility to Only me.
On Desktop
  1. Open instagram.com → More → Settings → Accounts Center.
  2. Choose Personal details → Contact info.
  3. Confirm your recovery contacts and hide them from your public profile.

Tips: A private, up-to-date recovery email helps you regain access if your account is locked.

Make Your Instagram Account Private

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy.
  2. Under Who can see your content, tap Account privacy.
  3. Toggle Private account on and confirm.
On Desktop
  1. Go to instagram.com → More → Settings.
  2. Select Privacy in the left column.
  3. Check Private account and confirm the change.

Tips: Only approved followers will see your posts, stories, and followers list.

Limit Who Can Reply to Your Stories

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy → Messages and story replies.
  2. Tap Story replies.
  3. Choose People you follow or Off to restrict replies.
On Desktop
  1. instagram.com → More → Settings → Privacy.
  2. Select Messages and story replies → Story replies.
  3. Pick who can reply to your stories.

Tips: Limiting replies reduces unwanted DMs triggered by stories.

Control Instagram Message Requests

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy → Messages and story replies.
  2. Tap Message controls.
  3. Adjust Potential connections and Others on Instagram to send requests to the Don't receive requests folder or turn them off entirely.
On Desktop
  1. In Settings, choose Privacy → Messages.
  2. Set each category (People you follow, Others, Facebook friends) to the strictest option available.

Tips: Tight message controls limit spam and reduce phishing attempts in your inbox.

Hide Your Instagram Activity Status

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy → Messages and story replies.
  2. Tap Show activity status and toggle it off.
On Desktop
  1. instagram.com → More → Settings.
  2. Choose Privacy → Messages → toggle off Show activity status.

Tips: Hiding activity status prevents contacts from seeing when you were last active.

Remove Connected Apps on Instagram

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy.
  2. Scroll to How others can interact with you and tap Apps and websites.
  3. Review Active apps, tap any you do not use, then tap Remove.
On Desktop
  1. instagram.com → More → Settings → Apps and websites.
  2. Check the Active tab, click Remove for apps you do not trust.

Tips: Removing old integrations stops them from accessing your profile data.

Adjust Instagram Ad Topics

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy → Ads.
  2. Tap Ad topics.
  3. Choose sensitive topics and select See less.
On Desktop
  1. In Settings, select Ads → Ad topics.
  2. Set each topic you want to limit to See less.

Tips: Adjusting ad topics reduces targeting based on sensitive interests.

Download Your Instagram Data

On Mobile
  1. Profile → Menu → Settings and privacy → Accounts Center.
  2. Tap Your information and permissions → Download your information.
  3. Select Instagram, choose the date range and format, then tap Request a download.
On Desktop
  1. instagram.com → More → Settings → Accounts Center.
  2. Go to Your information and permissions → Download your information.
  3. Pick Instagram data, set your filters, and click Request a download.

Tips: Reviewing your archive helps you decide what to delete or tighten in privacy controls.
